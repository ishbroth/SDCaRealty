What's the first rule in real estate? San Diego Rules.

A truly multi-cultural metro area packed with energy, amenities, activities and scenery - beaches, deserts, hiking trails through the hills, urban condos, fenced vineyards and horse farms - all basking in a warm Mediterranean climate conveniently located right next to the great Pacific Ocean.

Gaslamp Quarter

San Diego's nightlife scene, 3 blocks wide and 16 blocks long, packed with high-rises and some of California's best restaurants, bars, clubs and venues.

Mission Beach

A unique 2-mile-long pedestrian-based community with one main road, built on a sand bar between the Pacific Ocean and Mission Bay.

North Park

Major Williamsburg, Brooklyn or Wicker Park, Chicago vibes here. A denser main corridor offering an urban feel.

Pacific Beach

This is where nightlife comes with an ocean view. Two main streets, Grand and Garnet Ave., provide bars, clubs and restaurants.

Hillcrest

Features "fabulous and quirky" character around Balboa Park's northwest corner.

Ocean Beach

Where the bohemian style lives on with eclectic dining and entertainment.

La Jolla

Known as "The Jewel" featuring upscale dining and chic shopping.

Little Italy

Features upscale dining and spacious piazzas interwoven into the climbing hills.

Downtown/Columbia

Contains highrises, banks, government buildings, navy ships with condos offering panoramic views.

Old Town

Perhaps the most historical in the state considering it's known as the birthplace of California.

Point Loma

One of the original communities of Southern California where homes ALL come with a spectacular view of either the ocean or the bay.

Coronado

If La Jolla is the jewel, then Coronado is literally "The Crown" on an island community within San Diego Bay.

La Mesa

Dubbed the "Jewel of the Hills" with a family-oriented suburban feel.

Banker's Hill

A San Francisco-esque walkable hip urban area packed with pedestrian bridges, bike lanes.

Golden Hill

Features old victorian style homes as well as modern condos teeming with youthful professionals.

Normal Heights

A neighborhood in its own little pocket surrounded by highways but with convenient local amenities.

Chula Vista

The spiciest city north of the border is also the largest suburb of San Diego.

Mission Hills

Reminiscent of Beverly Hills, except way less exposure.

Kensington

Single-family homes are abundant and so are amenities.

Burlingame/South Park

Another quaint residential gem, centered around tree-lined Fern street.

Imperial Beach

Bordering Tijuana is the most south-westernly community in the U.S. of A.

University City

Centered around UCSD functioning as an "Uptown" San Diego.

Liberty Station

An ex-military base converted into a walkable plaza filled with shops, local artisans.

Mission Valley

Popular with students, young pros, and military personnel featuring two malls and trolley access.

Bird Rock

A small, closely-knit neighborhood between Pacific Beach and La Jolla.

Del Cerro

A well-to-do neighborhood in east-central San Diego overlooking the campus of San Diego State University.

East Village

An up-and-coming side of San Diego's downtown with breweries and new lofts.

San Carlos

Sandwiched between the Lake Murray reservoir and the stunning and hikeable mission trails.

Lemon Grove

An inner-ring suburb experiencing significant revitalization.

Allied Gardens

Located in the mission gorge area offering great views and range from affordable to quite pricey.

Poway

"The city in the country", this inland northeast San Diego suburb offers a little more land for your money.

Spring Valley

Offering suburban living sprawling through the hills of near-east county.

Carmel Valley/Rancho PQ

The large, somewhat remote, somewhat pricey area that covers the furthest northern reaches.

El Cajon

"East County" with starter homes and multi-unit properties in the central valley.

Lakeside

Sits by the side of not one, but three fish-stocked lakes.

Tierrasanta

"The island in the hills", this little enclave of San Diego borders Mission Trails Park.

Mira Mesa

Northern enclaves of San Diego with over 100K residents.

Linda Vista

Peaceful residential neighborhood northeast of USD with wonderful views overlooking Mission Bay.

Del Mar

Dreamy coastal suburb hosting the Del Mar Fairgrounds and horse racing.

Encinitas

Another idyllic beachside village featuring Leucadia and Cardiff-by-the-Sea.

Carlsbad/Oceanside

Picturesque SoCal oceanfront vista. Boardwalks and Piers give way to eateries and local shops.

Escondido

Rapidly growing inland suburb and one of the oldest cities in California.
